# Skill: cgv

**Agent**: Jules (Legal & Compliance)
**Priority**: P0

## Purpose

Generate CGV (Conditions Générales de Vente) and CGU (Conditions Générales d'Utilisation) adapted to a SaaS business model.

## CGV structure (B2B SaaS)

1. **Object** — what the service provides
2. **Subscription** — how plans work, what's included
3. **Pricing** — prices, VAT, annual vs. monthly
4. **Trial** — trial duration, what happens at end
5. **Payment** — accepted methods, invoicing, late payment
6. **Cancellation** — how to cancel, refund policy (typically none for SaaS)
7. **Service availability** — SLA commitments (or lack thereof for beta)
8. **Data ownership** — user owns their data
9. **Intellectual property** — who owns what
10. **Liability limitation** — cap at 12 months of fees paid
11. **Governing law** — France, French courts

## CGU structure

1. **Acceptable use** — what users can/cannot do
2. **Account responsibilities** — user responsible for their account
3. **Prohibited content** — specific list (not vague "inappropriate content")
4. **Suspension/termination** — when and how we can terminate
5. **Third-party integrations** — disclaimer for third-party services

## Guard-rails

- Never promise SLA uptime you can't deliver (99.9% = 8.7h downtime/year)
- Liability cap must be explicit — unlimited liability kills solo founders
- Refund policy: clearly state "no refunds for SaaS subscriptions" or specify exceptions
- Free trial terms: what happens to data if they don't convert
